# Market — Competitive Landscape & Positioning

## Market Size (France)

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: 27M daily car commuters in France → €80B+ annual commuting spend
- **SAM (Serviceable)**: ~8M rural/periurban-to-city commuters along identifiable corridors
- **SOM (Obtainable — Year 1)**: Loire-Atlantique corridor commuters → ~50,000 people → ~€15M annual commuting spend

## Existing Players

### BlaBlaCar
- **What they do**: Long-distance intercity carpooling (Paris→Lyon, etc.)
- **Why they don't solve our problem**: Designed for one-off trips, not daily commutes. No schedule matching. No corridor optimization. Urban-to-urban focus.
- **Their daily commute attempt** (BlaBlaLines → BlaBlaCar Daily): Shut down / minimal traction. One-off matching, no weekly intelligence.

### Karos
- **What they do**: Daily carpooling with employer partnerships
- **Gap**: B2B model (sells to companies), not community-driven. Requires employer onboarding. Doesn't handle variable schedules well.

### Klaxit (now BlaBlaCar Daily)
- **What they do**: Short-distance carpooling, subsidized by local authorities
- **Gap**: Real-time matching (Uber-like model), not weekly planning. Requires critical mass of simultaneous online users — hard in rural areas.

### Mobicoop
- **What they do**: Cooperative carpooling platform
- **Gap**: Basic matching, no AI optimization, low UX quality, cooperative governance = slow iteration.

## Key Insight

Everyone else is building **Uber for short distances** — real-time, on-demand, requiring critical mass of simultaneous users. That fails in rural areas because there aren't enough people online at the same moment.

We're building **weekly meal prep for commuting** — plan ahead, match in batch, optimize over the full week. This works in rural areas because you don't need simultaneous online users, just declared schedules.

## Station F / Fighter Program Positioning

**Narrative**: "BlaBlaCar solved intercity. No one has solved the daily rural commute. We use AI to match variable schedules — the exact reason informal carpooling fails. We start with one corridor, prove 20% adoption cuts traffic and costs by 66%, then scale nationally."

**Key metrics to demonstrate**:
- Simulation showing traffic reduction on real corridor
- User research/interviews from the Ligné→Nantes corridor
- Working MVP with real schedule matching
- Unit economics: cost per match, projected LTV

## Revenue Model (Future — Not MVP)

1. **Freemium**: Free basic matching, premium for priority matching + analytics
2. **Local authority partnerships**: Cities pay per car removed from road (precedent: Klaxit model)
3. **Employer partnerships**: Companies subsidize employee carpooling (tax incentive: Forfait Mobilités Durables up to €700/year tax-free)
4. **Data licensing**: Anonymized commuting pattern data for urban planners

## Regulatory Tailwinds (France)

- **LOM (Loi d'Orientation des Mobilités 2019)**: Mandates local authorities to organize shared mobility
- **Forfait Mobilités Durables**: €700/year tax-free employer subsidy for carpooling
- **ZFE (Zones à Faibles Émissions)**: Low-emission zones pushing people toward shared transport
- **France Nation Verte**: Government climate plan prioritizing transport decarbonization
