### Application Strategy

**Problem Statement**:
"27 million French people drive alone to work every day. In rural areas, carpooling fails because variable schedules make coordination impossible. We use AI to batch-match commuters weekly, turning schedule chaos into optimized carpool groups."

**Why Now**:
- AI costs collapsed → intelligent matching is now feasible at scale
- French regulation (LOM, FMD) actively pushing shared mobility
- Post-COVID variable schedules killed traditional carpooling → our approach is designed for this

**Traction to Show**:

- Matching algorithm that demonstrably reduces traffic 20-40%
- User interviews from target corridor
- Technical MVP (schedule input → matched groups)

## Phase 2: Local Pilot (Month 1-3 at Station F)

### Strategy: Corridor-by-Corridor

1. **Pick ONE corridor** (Ligné→Nantes)
2. **Door-to-door outreach**: literally go to the villages, talk to people
3. **Partner with mairies** (town halls): they have community email lists
4. **Facebook groups**: "Habitants de Ligné", "Saint-Mars du Désert" — these exist and are active
5. **Boulangeries & supermarchés**: QR code posters at community boards
6. **Target**: 100 active users on one corridor within 3 months

### Critical Mass Calculation
- Corridor has ~7,800 commuters
- Need ~50 commuters in each time slot to generate good matches
- With 5 time slots (6h30, 7h00, 7h30, 8h00, 8h30) → need ~250 people minimum
- At 40% carpool-willing → need ~625 people registered
- So target: **500-1000 registrations** on first corridor, **200+ active weekly**

## Phase 3: Nantes Multi-Corridor (Month 4-12)
